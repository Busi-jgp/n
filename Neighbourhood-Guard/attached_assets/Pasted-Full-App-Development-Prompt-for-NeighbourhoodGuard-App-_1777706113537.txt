Full App Development Prompt for NeighbourhoodGuard
App Name: NeighbourhoodGuard
Tagline: Hyper-local safety awareness for your neighbourhood.
App Concept
NeighbourhoodGuard is a hyper-local, community-powered safety mobile app designed for South African suburbs and estates (starting in Pretoria/Gauteng). It helps residents stay aware of safety conditions in real time and make safer decisions when walking or commuting.
Core Features (MVP Priority)

Real-time Safety Map – Interactive map showing live incidents, suspicious activity, protests, load-shedding areas, and road hazards in the user’s exact neighbourhood.
Quick Community Alerts – Verified nearby users (within 500m – 2km radius) can send fast one-tap or short alerts (e.g. “Suspicious person”, “Car break-in in progress”, “Unsafe route”) with optional photo/video. Alerts should only be visible to users in the same local area.
Live Safest Route Guidance – Dynamic walking/driving routes that automatically avoid recent incidents and high-risk zones, combining community reports with traffic data.
Neighbourhood Feed – Local safety updates with the ability to post “All Clear” reports.
User Check-in – “I’m walking home” feature with optional live location sharing to trusted contacts.

Key Differentiators

Strictly hyper-local focus (not city-wide or national).
Prevention and awareness rather than only panic-button emergency response.
Strong emphasis on user verification, low noise, and trust.

Target Users
Residents in middle to high-income suburbs, gated estates, and neighbourhoods in Gauteng (Pretoria and Johannesburg areas).
Technical Requirements

Cross-platform: Flutter or React Native (both Android & iOS).
Real-time backend: Firebase or Supabase (with WebSockets for live alerts).
Maps & Routing: Google Maps API or Mapbox (with dynamic routing and overlays).
Location: Precise GPS + geofencing.
Authentication: Phone number OTP + neighbourhood/address verification.
Offline support: Cache maps and queue reports when data is limited.
Security: Data encryption, secure image/video upload, and strong moderation queue for alerts.
Scalability: Must handle growth from one suburb to multiple estates.

Design & User Experience

Clean, calm, and trustworthy interface (avoid fear-based red colours as default).
Simple and fast to use, even under stress.
Dark/light mode support.

Monetization (to be implemented after MVP)

Freemium model: Basic features free, Premium subscription (suggested R49 – R79 per month) for advanced safest routes, unlimited alerts, live tracking, and ad-free experience.
B2B: Sell group licences to residential estates, body corporates, and neighbourhood watches (R200 – R500 per month per estate).
Partnerships: Commission from private security companies and insurance providers.

Compliance & Legal

Full POPIA compliance (consent management, data minimisation, privacy policy).
Clear disclaimers: User-generated alerts are not officially verified.
Moderation system to reduce false reports, abuse, and vigilantism.
